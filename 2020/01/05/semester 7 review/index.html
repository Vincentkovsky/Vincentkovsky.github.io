IPM review

Decision making

Decision making styles

  1. Autocratic- using information available at the time
  2. Information seeking- obtain information from subordinates
  3. Consulting- share problem with subordinates
  4. Negotiating- collect subordinates’ ideas and suggestions
  5. Group- reach consensus

Group decision making

  1. Advantages: More complete information available; better buy-in(买入价格); increase legitimacy（决策合理性）.
  2. Disadvantages: Time consuming; minority domination(people are not equal in the decision making process); pressure to conform.

Symptoms of groupthink

  1. Illusion of invulnerability- any decision we make will be successful
  2. Belief in morality of the group- justifying a decision by reference to a higher value
  3. Rationalisation- playing down the negative consequences of risks
  4. Stereotyping out-groups- referring to opponents in unfavourable or dismissive terms

Earned Value Analysis: a comprehensive time and cost trend analysis and prediction. It combines time, cost and production data.

  1. BCWS- Budgeted cost of work scheduled(Budgeted cost); BCWP- Budgeted cost of work performed(earned value); ACWP- Actual cost of work performed(Actual cost)
  2. Cost performance index(CPI)= BCWP/ ACWP
  3. Schedule performance index(SPI)= BCWP/BCWS
  4. Estimated cost at completion= original budget/CPI
  5. Estimated time at completion= original time estimate/SPI
  6. Cost variance= BCWP- ACWP
  7. Schedule variance= BCWP- BCWS

Accounting rate of return (ARR) : required return an investment should achieve

  1. ARR= average profit(after depreciation)/average investment
  2. Avg investment=(investment +disposal value)/2

Internal rate of return: discount rate that makes net present value equal to 0

  1. bigger than the discount rate, the project is profitable; vice versa.

Intellectual property and ownership

  1. Forms of IP protection: copyright; patents; registered designs; trademarks
  2. IP refers to creations of the mind, such as inventions; literary and artistic works; designs and symbols, names and images used in commerce. IP is protected in law by, for example, patents, copyright and trademarks, which enable people to earn recognition or financial benefit from what they invent or create.
  3. IP should be learned because: we need to understand how we can protect things we have created such as designs, solutions, text, etc; we need to check if our designs, solutions, text, etc. don’t infringe others’ IP rights; we need to understand different forms of IP protection, the process of registering IP and what action we can take to defend our IP.
  4. Registered designs: protect any aspects of your design(appearance, configuration, shape and decoration); prevent others from using it for up to 25 years; makes legal action against infringement and copying more straightforward; display registration number on your design.

Project risk management(PRM)

Risk: the combination of the probability of an undesired event occurring and the degree of impact upon a project

Risk management: identifying potential risks; seeing if they can be eliminated at the planning stage; having plans for dealing with them.

Team Development

stages

  1. Forming: the stage at which members choose, or are told, to join a team. They come together and begin to find out who the members are, and share superficial information about themselves, and also begin to offer ideas about how the group should work. People are trying to impress the group and establish their identity with others.
  2. Storming: Conflicts may occur at this stage. As the group begins the actual work, members begin to express differences of interest that they withheld, or didn’t recognise at the forming stage. People realise that others want different things from the group, have other priorities and, have hidden agendas. Contrasting attitudes towards the group and how it should work emerge. Differences in values become clear.
  3. Norming: In this stage, members begin to accommodate differences constructively and establish adequate ways of working together. They develop a set of shared norms(how they should interact, approach the task, deal with differences). Roles are set and accepted.
  4. Performing: The group gets on with the job to the required standard and achieves its objectives.
  5. Adjourning: The team completes its task and disbands. Members may reflect on how the group performed and identify lessons for future tasks.

Sales& Negotiation

phases of negotiation

  1. Planning: ensure deadlines; prepare all moves; establish target objective; appraise target objective; select negotiation strategy; evaluate information; assess risks.
  2. Opening: submit offer/answer: verbal discussion; face to face discussion; verbally at the meeting.
  3. Follow-up: each side starts to adjust significantly demand and attitude upon observed behavior of opponent; keep the negotiation team united; time may add pressure to opponent.
  4. Identifying Bargain: signaling to opponent: final positions, no loose end, no other way out.
  5. Concluding Bargain: final review, concession; ensure a genuine agreement(price, completion); record the bargain.
